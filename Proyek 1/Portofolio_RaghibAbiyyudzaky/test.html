List History

World War 1

World War 1 (1914–1918) was a major global conflict involving many countries across Europe, the Middle East, Africa, and Asia. The war was triggered by the assassination of Archduke Franz Ferdinand of Austria on June 28, 1914.

World War 2

World War 2 (1939–1945) was the deadliest and most widespread conflict in human history, involving over 70 countries across Europe, Asia, Africa, and the Pacific. It was primarily fought between two opposing military alliances: Allies and Axis.

Crusade War

The Crusades were a series of religious wars between the 11th and 15th centuries, primarily initiated by European Christians against Muslim forces in the Holy Land. The main goal was to reclaim Jerusalem and other sacred territories from Muslim control.

Cold War

The Cold War (1947–1991) was a prolonged period of geopolitical tension between the United States (USA) and the Soviet Union (USSR), along with their respective allies in the Western and Eastern Blocs. It was characterized by ideological, political, military, and economic rivalry.

Operation Dessert Storm

Operation Desert Storm (January 17 – February 28, 1991) was the combat phase of the Gulf War, launched by a U.S.-led coalition against Iraq in response to its invasion of Kuwait. It was a decisive military campaign aimed at liberating Kuwait and weakening Iraqi military capabilities.

Vietnam War

The Vietnam War was a prolonged conflict between the communist North Vietnam, supported by the Soviet Union and China, and South Vietnam, backed primarily by the United States and its allies. It was part of the larger Cold War struggle between communism and democracy.

Indonesian History

Indonesia, the world's largest archipelago, has a rich and diverse history. It was shaped by indigenous kingdoms, colonial rule, and struggles for independence. Over time, it transitioned from colonial occupation to a modern democracy. Today, it remains a key economic and political power in Southeast Asia.

Australian History

Australia has a rich and diverse history, beginning with its Indigenous peoples who have lived for over 60,000 years. British colonization in 1788 led to significant changes, including conflict and expansion. Over time, Australia evolved into an independent nation and a modern democracy. Today, it is a multicultural society with global influence.

6 Days War

The Six-Day War was a brief but decisive conflict fought between Israel and the Arab states of Egypt, Jordan, and Syria from June 5 to June 10, 1967. It reshaped the Middle East and significantly altered regional geopolitics. Israel achieved a swift victory, gaining control of the Sinai Peninsula, Gaza Strip, West Bank, East Jerusalem, and Golan Heights.

Reconquista

The Reconquista was a centuries-long series of battles in the Iberian Peninsula, where Christian kingdoms fought to reclaim territory from Muslim Moors who had ruled since 711 AD. It lasted from 718 to 1492, shaping Spain and Portugal’s history. The conflict ended with the conquest of Granada by Ferdinand and Isabella in 1492, unifying Spain under Catholic rule.

French Revolutionary

The French Revolution (1789–1799) was a period of political and social upheaval that led to the overthrow of the monarchy and the rise of democracy in France. Driven by economic hardship, social inequality, and Enlightenment ideas, it saw key events like the Storming of the Bastille and the Reign of Terror, and the revolution ended with Napoleon Bonaparte's rise to power in 1799.

Napoleonic War

The Napoleonic Wars (1803–1815) were a series of conflicts between Napoleon’s French Empire and various European coalitions. Napoleon achieved major victories, expanding French rule, but faced setbacks in the Peninsular War (1808–1814) and the disastrous Russian Campaign (1812). He was defeated at Leipzig (1813), finally lost at Waterloo (1815).
